Overview

Designed and manufactured a small-scale wind turbine using a NACA 4415 airfoil to optimize aerodynamic efficiency, structural stability, and manufacturability.

Challenges

  • Integrating the NACA 4415 airfoil while balancing lift, drag, and structural constraints.
  • Designing blade geometry and chord distribution for efficient airflow capture.
  • Ensuring proper fit, tolerances, and alignment for reliable assembly and testing.

Outcome

  • Successfully fabricated and assembled a functional wind turbine.
  • Achieved stable operation and consistent performance during testing.
  • Delivered a compact, manufacturable design that met project requirements.
